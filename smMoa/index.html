History of the Mall of Asia

The Mall of Asia (MoA) is one of the largest shopping malls in the Philippines, opened to the public in 2006. Located in Pasay City, it is a shopping destination as well as a cultural and entertainment hub. Developed by SM Prime Holdings, MoA features over 600 retail shops, a wide variety of restaurants, an IMAX theater, and an amusement park. It is a significant landmark in Metro Manila and has hosted numerous events, concerts, and exhibitions.

MoA is composed of four interconnected buildings: the Main Mall, Entertainment Mall, and the North and South Parking areas, connected via walkways for convenience. It used to be the biggest mall in the country before the expansions of SM North Edsa and SM Megamall. MoA is also known for its spacious parking and iconic globe made of steel frames and LED lights.

The mall is home to the first Olympic-sized ice skating rink in Southeast Asia, the first-ever IMAX theater in the Philippines, and the SM By The Bay amusement park, offering plenty of activities inside and outside the mall for all visitors to enjoy.
